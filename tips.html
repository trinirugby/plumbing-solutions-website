Kitchen

Garbage Disposal & Kitchen Plumbing

  • Do run cold water while the disposer is on, and for a few seconds after — it flushes waste fully into the drain line.
  • Don't put fibrous or starchy waste (celery, potato peels, banana peels) down the disposer — it can jam the blades.
  • Clean the disposer periodically with ice cubes and citrus rinds — the ice sharpens the blades and the citrus cuts odor.
  • If your disposer overloads and trips, hit the reset button on the underside of the unit, then try again on a lower load.
  • Soak faucet aerators in vinegar periodically to clear mineral buildup and restore full water flow.

Bathroom

Bathroom Maintenance

  • Flush your hot water heater weekly to clear sediment and keep it running efficiently.
  • Fix leaky faucets promptly — a small drip wastes far more water than it looks like.
  • Use drain strainers in the shower and sink to keep hair and debris out of your lines.
  • Descale your shower head periodically by soaking it in a bag of vinegar overnight.
  • Never use the toilet as a wastebasket — wipes, cotton, and other debris are a leading cause of clogs.
  • Test your toilet for a silent leak: add a few drops of food coloring to the tank and check the bowl after 15 minutes without flushing.
  • Clean aerators on bathroom faucets regularly with vinegar to maintain water pressure.

Save Water, Save Money

Water Conservation

Watch Your Pressure

Keep household water pressure under 60 PSI with a pressure-reducing valve — high pressure wastes water and stresses fittings.

Test For Hidden Leaks

Turn off every fixture in the house, then check your water meter. If it's still moving, you likely have a hidden leak.

Toilets Use The Most

Toilets account for 40%+ of household water use — a low-flow toilet pays for itself quickly.

Lower The Water Heater

Set your water heater to 120°F (about 49°C) — hot enough for comfort, cool enough to save energy.

Low-Flow Shower Heads

A low-flow shower head can save up to 7.5 gallons per minute compared to an older fixture.

Fix That Drip

A single dripping faucet can waste more than 3,000 gallons a year — call us before it adds up.
